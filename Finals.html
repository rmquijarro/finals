Yellow Tulips

Yellow tulips symbolize cheerfulness, happiness, and friendship. They convey a sense of warmth and positivity.

Pink Tulips

Pink tulips signify affection, caring, and good wishes. They express a softer form of love compared to red tulips.

Red Tulips

Red tulips are the quintessential symbol of love, representing deep passion and romantic feelings.

Purple Tulips

Purple tulips symbolize royalty, nobility, elegance, and admiration. They also represent sincerity in love and spirituality. These meanings make purple tulips suitable for expressing deep respect and appreciation on various occasions.

Other types of Tulips

Type of Tulip Description Bloom Time Characteristics
Single Early Cup-shaped flowers with bright colors. Early Spring Short stems (up to 16 inches), sturdy, suitable for cool climates.
Triumph Traditional tulip shape with a wide range of colors. Mid-Season Medium-length stems (16-20 inches), strong and upright.
Darwin Hybrid Large, oval blooms on tall, sturdy stems. Mid-Spring Known for vibrant colors and longevity; ideal for mass planting and cut flowers.
Lily-Flowered Elegant flowers with long, pointed petals creating a distinctive profile. Late Spring Graceful appearance, excellent for bedding and cutting.
Parrot Ruffled and twisted petals in vibrant colors, resembling exotic birds' feathers. Late Spring Flamboyant appearance; often multi-colored and dramatic in gardens.
